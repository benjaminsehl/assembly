# Business Model Evaluation

## Workflow

Evaluate the product as a business hypothesis:

1. Identify the user, buyer, budget owner, and buying trigger.
2. Name the expensive pain or strategic upside.
3. Compare current alternatives and why switching might happen now.
4. Propose pricing, packaging, and the first distribution channel.
5. Identify retention, expansion, and cost-to-serve risks.
6. Name the moat or compounding advantage if the product works.
7. Define evidence that would validate or kill the business case.

Use `references/business-model-checklist.md` for the rubric when available.

## Output

Return:

- ICP and buyer.
- Willingness-to-pay hypothesis.
- Distribution hypothesis.
- Retention loop.
- Biggest business risk.
- Next validation step.

## Verification

- At least one assumption is falsifiable.
- The next evidence step does not require overbuilding.
- Business viability is separated from product usefulness.
