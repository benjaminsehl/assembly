# Founder Review

## Purpose

Challenge whether the work is solving the right problem at the right level of ambition. Look for the sharper wedge, the lovable product moment, and the scope that can actually ship.

## Underlying skills

- `founder-product-critique`
- `idea-refine`
- `planning-and-task-breakdown`

## Workflow

1. State that `founder-review` is active and identify the artifact being reviewed.
2. Use `founder-product-critique` to evaluate user urgency, ambition, differentiation, and scope.
3. Use `idea-refine` when the plan needs stronger alternatives or sharper positioning.
4. If the plan is too broad, use `planning-and-task-breakdown` to recommend a smaller learning slice.
5. Check `references/product-discovery-checklist.md` for pressure-test questions.
6. Output a verdict: Expand, Narrow, Hold scope, or Stop.
7. Include the top changes that would make the product more loved or more viable.

## Verification

- The verdict is explicit.
- The review names what should change and what should stay fixed.
- User-love, feasibility, and business implications are all addressed.
- Any recommended expansion includes a smaller first slice.

## Stop Conditions

- The artifact has no clear target user or success criteria.
- The recommended change would invalidate user-approved scope without asking.
- The product risk is really an engineering, security, or launch blocker that belongs in another workflow.
