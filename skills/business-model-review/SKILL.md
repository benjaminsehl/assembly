# Business Model Review

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a product can plausibly become a viable business, not just a useful feature. Convert assumptions into testable business hypotheses.

## Underlying skills

- `business-model-evaluation`
- `idea-refine`
- `documentation-and-adrs`

## Workflow

1. State that `business-model-review` is active and identify the product concept or feature.
2. Use `business-model-evaluation` to assess ICP, pain intensity, alternatives, willingness to pay, distribution, pricing, retention, cost risk, and moat.
3. Use `idea-refine` to compare business-model options when multiple motions are plausible.
4. Check `references/business-model-checklist.md` for the review rubric.
5. Document the strongest business hypothesis, weakest assumption, and next evidence-gathering step.
6. If a build is recommended, name the smallest product slice that can validate the business assumption.

## Verification

- ICP and buyer/user distinction are clear.
- Pricing or willingness-to-pay hypothesis is explicit.
- Distribution channel and retention loop are named.
- The next evidence step can falsify at least one important assumption.

## Stop Conditions

- There is no plausible user or buyer.
- The feature has value but no credible monetization or strategic reason.
- Business assumptions are being treated as proven without evidence.
