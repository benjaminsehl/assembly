# Retro

## Purpose

Close the loop after building. Capture shipped outcomes, user learning, engineering lessons, business signals, and process improvements.

## Underlying skills

- `documentation-and-adrs`
- `code-review-and-quality`
- `business-model-evaluation`
- `founder-product-critique`

## Workflow

1. State that `retro` is active and identify the time period, release, or project being reviewed.
2. Gather shipped changes, incidents, test/build health, user feedback, usage signals, and business outcomes.
3. Use `founder-product-critique` to review whether the work improved user love.
4. Use `business-model-evaluation` to review business signal and assumption changes.
5. Use `code-review-and-quality` for engineering lessons and quality gaps.
6. Use `documentation-and-adrs` to recommend durable notes or ADR updates.
7. Output wins, misses, learnings, decisions, and next actions.

## Verification

- Shipped work and observed outcomes are separated from guesses.
- Product, engineering, and business lessons are all covered.
- Follow-up actions have owners or clear next triggers.
- Durable lessons are identified without overwriting memory systems.

## Stop Conditions

- There is no release, period, or artifact to review.
- The retro would require private analytics or user data that is not available.
- The requested output should be a formal memory update and the user has not asked for one.
